Another Year

Days pass by like flowing water.

The year is ending. Time flows like an unstoppable river, crushing everything within its way until it reaches the ocean. I am 23 years old. The average life expectancy for a male in my demographic is around 72 years old. Without realizing it, I am nearly halfway there. Halfway to an ending. My own father met death at the ripe age of 59. Perhaps I too might only be able to make it to that age. Or perhaps even that brief longetivity is far too distant to grasp.

Resolutions

With the advent of the new year, motivations run high. Fuelled by wishes, dreams and hopes, people set their minds on trying to better themselves, idealogized by the zeitgeist phrase “New Year, New Me!”. All too often though, the resolutions that they pledge; to be fitter, healthier, smarter, stronger, more successful, fail within the first month. It is the nature of the human being to be resistant to change, to stay inside their comfort zone. Venturing out of the circle of amenities is the only time where a person truly discovers themselves. I myself am not immune to this phenomena. And it is thus that I pledge my resolution for this year.

I vow to:

  • Achieve fitness goals and get bigger.
  • Maybe find a partner, as I would like to fill a lonely void.
  • Be wiser, smarter, and read more, preferably on philosophy.
  • Accelerate my career and earn a decent living, enough to support myself and give to my precious mother.
  • Embody goodness. Be free and caring.
  • Make an absolutely beautiful aquascape for a betta fish.

Wandering down the road of life, stop by for a roadside picnic, and admire the view at worlds end.
